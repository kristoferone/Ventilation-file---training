Mental fatigue is an issue that all athletes face. Yes, I'm probably physically tired as well, but it's the mental side that is more difficult. There's a couple of things that really help with the mental side and motivation. Planning is definitely the best way to keep on track. Confusion and indecision are very taxing mentally. Making decisions is generally a cost on your mental and cognitive resources. That's why we should make decisions ahead of time as much as possible. As Shearer says, avoid the enemies of concentration: anxiety, confusion, boredom, and discomfort. Some discomfort is inherent to exercise and actually the point of it, but there's a distinction between your lungs and legs burning discomfort and your crotch chaffing. One is productive, the other not. Feed-forward, or projecting oneself into the future to a desirable outcome or state is also important. We always have explicit and implicit goals in our behaviour. Sometimes their ethereal and only appear in our minds transitorily. It's important to use visualization and imagery to sharpen those goals and make them more substantial. The more real that future image is for you, the easier it is to stay focused when you're feeling tired and frustrated.
Nevertheless, I am doing well with my training. One of the hardest things is to stay committed to the plan that you've created. It's easy to keep changing and end-gaining as you chase insubstantial and evaporating goals.
